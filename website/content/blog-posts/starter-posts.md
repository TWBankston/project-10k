## Post 1: Website Mistakes

**Title:** 5 Website Mistakes Costing Home Service Businesses Jobs  
**Slug:** /blog/website-mistakes-home-service-businesses  
**Meta Description:** Is your website losing you customers? Here are the 5 most common website mistakes we see from landscapers, cleaners, and home service pros.  
**Category:** Website Tips  
**Target Keywords:** home service website mistakes, landscaping website problems, contractor website tips

---

### Introduction

Your website is often the first impression potential customers have of your business. And if we're being honest? Most home service websites are costing their owners money every single day.

We've reviewed hundreds of websites from landscapers, cleaners, pest control companies, and other home service businesses. The same problems show up again and again.

Here are the 5 biggest website mistakes we see — and how to fix them.

---

### Mistake #1: Your Phone Number is Hidden

**The Problem:**
When someone lands on your website, they often want to call right away. But too many home service websites bury the phone number at the bottom of the page, or worse — only put it on the Contact page.

**The Fix:**
Your phone number should be visible on every page, at the top of the site. On mobile (where most people search), it should be a click-to-call button. Make it impossible to miss.

---

### Mistake #2: Your Site Doesn't Work on Mobile

**The Problem:**
Over 70% of local searches happen on mobile phones. If your website looks bad on a phone — text too small, buttons too close together, things overlapping — visitors will bounce instantly.

**The Fix:**
Make sure your site is responsive (automatically adjusts to screen size). Test it yourself: pull up your website on your phone. Is it easy to read? Easy to navigate? Easy to contact you?

---

### Mistake #3: No Photos of Your Work

**The Problem:**
You're a visual business. People want to see what you can do. But many home service websites use only stock photos — or worse, no photos at all.

**The Fix:**
Start taking photos of your work. Before and after shots are gold. Use your phone — you don't need a professional photographer. Real photos of real work build trust.

---

### Mistake #4: No Reviews or Testimonials

**The Problem:**
Trust is everything in home services. You're asking people to let you into their homes or onto their property. But many websites don't display any social proof.

**The Fix:**
Add a testimonials section to your homepage. Embed your Google reviews. If you don't have reviews yet, start asking for them after every job.

---

### Mistake #5: Slow Loading Speed

**The Problem:**
If your site takes more than 3 seconds to load, half your visitors will leave before seeing anything. Slow sites kill conversions and hurt your Google ranking.

**The Fix:**
Use compressed images, fast hosting, and avoid bloated page builders. Tools like Google PageSpeed Insights can tell you how your site performs.

---

### The Bottom Line

Your website should be your best salesperson — working 24/7 to bring in new customers. If you're making any of these mistakes, you're leaving money on the table.

## Post 2: Mobile-First Websites

**Title:** Why Home Service Businesses Need Mobile-First Websites in 2026  
**Slug:** /blog/mobile-first-websites-home-services  
**Meta Description:** Most of your customers search on their phones. Here's why mobile-first design is essential for landscapers, cleaners, and home service pros.  
**Category:** Website Tips  
**Target Keywords:** mobile website home services, responsive design contractor, mobile-friendly landscaping website

---

### Introduction

Think about the last time you searched for a local service. You probably grabbed your phone, typed something like "landscaper near me," and clicked a few results.

Your customers do the exact same thing.

In 2026, over 70% of local service searches happen on mobile devices. If your website doesn't work perfectly on a phone, you're losing jobs to competitors who figured this out.

---

### What "Mobile-First" Actually Means

Mobile-first design means your website is designed for phone screens first, then adapted for desktop — not the other way around.

This matters because when you design for desktop first, mobile often becomes an afterthought. Text gets too small. Buttons are too close together. Pages take forever to load.

Mobile-first flips this: phone experience is the priority.

---

### Why It Matters for Home Services

**1. Your customers search on phones**
When someone needs a landscaper, cleaner, or pest control service, they're rarely sitting at a computer. They're on their phone, often looking for help right now.

**2. Google ranks mobile-friendly sites higher**
Google uses "mobile-first indexing" — meaning they judge your site primarily based on the mobile version. Poor mobile experience = lower rankings.

**3. First impressions matter**
A clunky mobile experience signals unprofessionalism. If your website is frustrating to use, customers will assume your service might be too.

---

### Mobile-First Must-Haves

**Click-to-call phone number**
One tap to call you. No copying numbers, no dialing.

**Easy-to-read text**
16px minimum font size. No tiny text.

**Touch-friendly buttons**
Big enough to tap accurately. Spaced apart properly.

**Fast loading**
Under 3 seconds. Every second longer loses customers.

**Simple navigation**
Clear menu. Easy to find key info (services, contact, reviews).

**Form-friendly**
Contact forms that work on mobile. Proper keyboard types for phone/email fields.

---

### Test Your Site Right Now

1. Grab your phone
2. Go to your website
3. Ask yourself:
   - Can I easily find the phone number?
   - Can I navigate without zooming?
   - Does it load in under 3 seconds?
   - Is it easy to contact you?

If you answered "no" to any of these, you're losing potential customers.

---

### The Bottom Line

Mobile-first isn't optional anymore. It's essential.

The businesses winning in 2026 are the ones who meet customers where they are — on their phones, ready to book.

## Post 3: Google Reviews

**Title:** How to Get More Google Reviews for Your Home Service Business  
**Slug:** /blog/get-more-google-reviews-home-services  
**Meta Description:** Google Reviews are essential for home service businesses. Here's a simple system to get more 5-star reviews consistently.  
**Category:** Local SEO  
**Target Keywords:** google reviews home services, get more reviews landscaping, contractor review strategy

---

### Introduction

Here's a truth most home service business owners don't want to hear: potential customers check your Google reviews before they call you.

One business has 47 reviews with a 4.8 rating. Another has 3 reviews from 2019. Which one gets the call?

Reviews are social proof. They build trust. They help you rank higher in Google. And the good news? Getting more reviews isn't complicated — it just takes a system.

---

### Why Reviews Matter (Beyond the Obvious)

**They help you rank in Google Maps**
Google favors businesses with more (and better) reviews in local search results. More reviews = higher visibility.

**They build instant trust**
When someone considers letting you into their home or onto their property, reviews from real people provide reassurance.

**They pre-sell your services**
Great reviews do your selling for you. Customers arrive already confident in your work.

**They give you feedback**
Reviews tell you what you're doing right — and occasionally what you need to improve.

---

### The Simple Review System

**Step 1: Create your Google Review link**

Go to your Google Business Profile, click "Get more reviews," and copy your direct review link. This link takes customers straight to the review form.

**Step 2: Ask at the right moment**

The best time to ask is right after you've delivered great service. The customer is happy, the work is fresh in their mind, and they're most likely to say yes.

Say something simple:
> "If you were happy with our work, it would mean a lot if you could leave us a quick Google review. It really helps us out."

**Step 3: Make it easy**

Send a text or email with your direct review link. The fewer clicks required, the more reviews you'll get.

Example text:
> "Thanks for choosing [Company Name]! If you have a minute, we'd love a Google review: [link]"

**Step 4: Be consistent**

Don't just ask your favorite customers. Make asking for reviews part of your standard process after every completed job.

---

### Templates You Can Use

**Text message:**
> Hi [Name], thank you for choosing us! If you have a moment, a Google review helps us reach more homeowners like you: [link]

**Email:**
> Subject: Quick favor?
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for trusting us with your [service]. We hope we exceeded your expectations!
>
> If you have 60 seconds, we'd really appreciate a Google review. It helps other homeowners find us.
>
> [Leave a Review →]
>
> Thanks again,
> [Your name]

---

### How Many Reviews Do You Need?

There's no magic number, but here's a rough guide:

- **10+ reviews:** You're in the game
- **25+ reviews:** You look established
- **50+ reviews:** You stand out
- **100+ reviews:** You're a market leader

More importantly: reviews should be recent. A business with 50 reviews but none in the past 6 months looks stale.

---

### What About Bad Reviews?

Don't panic. Every business gets an occasional negative review. Here's how to handle it:

1. **Respond professionally** — Acknowledge the issue, apologize if appropriate, offer to make it right
2. **Don't argue** — You won't win, and future customers are watching
3. **Drown it out** — More positive reviews push negative ones down

A perfect 5.0 rating can actually look suspicious. A 4.7 with some variety looks authentic.

---

### The Bottom Line

Reviews are marketing that other people do for you. Create a system, ask consistently, and watch your reviews (and your business) grow.
