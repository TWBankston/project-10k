# Cold Call Script for Home Service Businesses

## Pre-Call Prep (30 seconds)
- Pull up their current website (or note they don't have one)
- Check Google reviews / rating
- Note their primary service area
- Have their name ready

---

## Opening Hook (10 seconds)

> **"Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with TBDesigned. I help [TRADE] companies in [AREA] turn their websites into booking machines. Got 60 seconds?"**

*If gatekeeper:* "I'm calling about their online presence — is [OWNER] available?"

---

## Quick Qualifying Questions (60-90 seconds)

**If they give you time:**

1. **"Quick question — when someone Googles '[their trade] near me,' are you showing up?"**
   - Gets them thinking about visibility

2. **"Where do most of your jobs come from right now?"**
   - Understand their current lead sources

3. **"When's the last time someone booked a job directly from your website?"**
   - This is the money question — most will say "I don't know" or "never"

---

## Value Pitch (30-45 seconds)

> **"Here's the thing, [NAME] — most contractors have websites that look fine but don't actually book jobs. They're basically online brochures.**
>
> **What we do is different. We build websites specifically designed to turn visitors into booked appointments. Click-to-call buttons, instant quote requests, the works. And we handle everything — you never touch it.**
>
> **Our clients typically see 3-5 new leads per week within the first month. That's real jobs, not just traffic."**

---

## Handling Objections

### "I get all my business from referrals"

> **"That's awesome — referrals mean you do great work. But here's the thing: even referrals Google you before they call. If your website looks outdated or you're hard to find, you're losing jobs you don't even know about.**
>
> **We had a plumber say the same thing — found out he was losing 30% of referrals because they couldn't find his number online. Would it hurt to have a second stream of leads backing up your referrals?"**

---

### "I already have a website"

> **"Great — is it booking you jobs? Like, actual appointments coming through it?**
>
> **[If no/unsure:]** Most contractor websites are just online business cards. They exist, but they don't work. We rebuild sites specifically to convert visitors into booked calls. Takes 2 weeks and we handle everything.**
>
> **[If yes:]** Nice! What's working well? [Listen, then] — Have you thought about what an extra 5-10 leads a month would do for your business?"**

---

### "That's too expensive"

> **"I hear you — what does one new job typically bring in for you?**
>
> **[Let them answer — usually $200-$2000+]**
>
> **So if your website brought in just 2-3 extra jobs a month, that's [$X] in revenue for $297. Most of our clients see ROI in the first week.**
>
> **Plus, there's no long-term contract trap — if it's not working after a few months, we'll know. But I've never had a client cancel because of lack of results."**

---

### "I'm too busy right now"

> **"That's actually the best time to set this up — when you're slow, you'll wish you had it running.**
>
> **Here's the good news: you don't have to do anything. We build it, we manage it, we update it. Takes maybe 30 minutes of your time total. You focus on the jobs, we focus on getting you more.**
>
> **Can I send you a quick example of what we'd build? Takes 2 minutes to look at."**

---

### "Let me think about it"

> **"Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? Is it the price, the timing, or just making sure it's worth it?**
>
> **[Address the real objection]**
>
> **Here's what I'd suggest — let's set up a 15-minute call for [specific day]. I'll show you exactly what we'd build for your business, no pressure. If it doesn't make sense, no hard feelings. Sound fair?"**

---

## Close for Meeting

> **"Look, I don't want to take more of your time on a cold call. Here's what I'd like to do:**
>
> **Let me send you a quick mockup of what your site could look like — takes me 10 minutes to put together. Then we jump on a 15-minute call, I walk you through it, and you decide if it makes sense.**
>
> **No pressure, no hard sell. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you. What's your email?"**

**Get email, then:**

> **"Perfect. I'll send that over today. Does [DAY] at [TIME] work for a quick call? Or [ALTERNATIVE]?"**

---

## After the Call

1. Send mockup/example within 2 hours
2. Calendar invite for follow-up
3. Add to CRM with notes
4. Follow-up email same day

## Voicemail Script (30 seconds max)

> **"Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with TBDesigned. I help [TRADE] companies turn their websites into booking machines.**
>
> **I took a look at your online presence and have a quick idea that could get you more jobs without spending money on ads.**
>
> **Give me a call back at [NUMBER] or I'll try you again [DAY]. Thanks!"**

---

## Key Principles

1. **Talk about THEM, not you** — their business, their problems, their goals
2. **Jobs, not websites** — they don't care about design, they care about money
3. **Be direct** — contractors respect straight talk
4. **Create urgency without pressure** — "slow season is coming" / "busy season is when you need backup leads"
5. **Get the meeting** — don't try to close on the first call
